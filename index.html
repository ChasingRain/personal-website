About Me

I spent most of my younger years making memories in Norris, Tennessee. We lived right down the street from the antique mall and East Tennessee's top Bluegrass station, WDVX.

As a kid, my parents loved taking us down the Clinch River which is where I initally fell in love with the river. I had no idea what impact those experiences would have on my life, but they have definitely led me down a path of adventure and exploration.

In high schoool, I got serious about gymnastics. Working up the ranks towards a college scholarship. In 2008 a Blue Chip recruit for the US Air Force Academy's gymnastics team, and eventually settled as a pommel horse specialist after multiple shoulder surgeries. Our sophomore year, the Air Force Academy won it's first USAG National Championchip. During that same competion I was also able to set the new AFA Pommel Horse Record.

During my sophomore year at the academy, I also fell in love with whitewater kayaking. I haad dreams to become a sponsored professional whitewater paddler, and eventually dropped out of the academy to pursue that dream. I eventually earned multiple kayaking sponsorships and finished my business management degree with a minor in entrepreneurship at the University of Colorado at Colorado Springs. During school I was coaching at a local gym and started my first company manufacturing gymnastics equipment. Living the dream.

After my last final exam I moved to Seattle with the love of my life and future wife, Kala. We got engaged during the move on a 100 mile river trip on the Middle Fork of the Salmon River in Idaho. Best decision I've ever made.

After moving to Seattle, I realized the job I had accepted was not what I was hoping for. I immediately started to work side a hustle. It was here, Fall 2013, when I met my current business partner, Mike Gordon. We started a cannabis tourism company called Kush Tourism which was the beginning of a long journey which has lead to our most exciting project, Kush Marketplace.

I have many stories I look forward to sharing, and I hope you enjoy them!
